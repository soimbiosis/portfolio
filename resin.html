Suspended

Personal project

In Brief

These pieces showcase two incredible types of plants that have no root structure: moss and seaweed. They require only sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide in order to photosynthesize. What if these could be living pieces one day?

Process

The seaweeds, moss, and mushrooms were collected and left to dry. EcoPoxy, a two-part epoxy, was used with ready-made silicone molds. Once cured, I sanded and polished them with a dremel, which I also used to drill holes into the pieces that became beads. Simple, solid shapes with clean lines complement the organic texture and form contained within and give the finished pieces a bold, sophisticated look.


Seaweeds suspended. Negative space complements their natural form and color, giving them an almost fluid appearance. The two seaweeds featured are cryptopleura ruprechtiana, commonly known as 'hidden rib', and ulva lactuca, also known as sea lettuce.


Moss collar necklace finished with an oxidized silver chain and photographed against driftwood.
