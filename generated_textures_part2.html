Generated Textures Part II: Temple of Texture

Personal Project

In Brief

In this part of my Generated Textures project, I move from 2D into 3D. I convert texture images synthesized in the first part of the project by my generative adversarial network into displacement maps, which I use to visualize the textures as 3D surfaces. Interpreting the texture images as displacements is only one way they could be used, just as generating textures as 2D images is only one way input information about textures, patterns, and materials can be used.

The vision of the pillars as a Temple of Texture was inspired in part by Michael Hansmeyer's Marchenwald installation.

Process

I selected output images from Part I that I could bring to life in 3D in my mind. I used CrazyBump to convert the images into displacement maps, which were imported into Blender and used as image textures.

I experimented with more complex base meshes, but opted in the end for simple columns as the displacement maps provided complexity and visual interest. The columns were UV-unwrapped and shaded using the displacement maps. A subdivision surface modifier was appliedto the entire column, followed by a displacement modifier applied only to the vertices along the vertical faces of the column.
