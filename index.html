Soimb·iosis

/ soi'imbīˈōsəs / • noun

A world where we are more than just consumers.
A world where we, like Mother Nature, design for the full lifecycle of every material, every product.
A world where we use technology to cultivate a symbiotic existence.
A world where we produce exactly what we need.
A world with less waste.

ORIGIN: Soimb from my ancestral language Konkani, meaning nature. -osis from Greek, denoting actions, conditions, or states.

About Me

I am an engineer and a creative on a mission to reduce waste. I received my B.S. in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon with a minor in Computational Biology. Though it didn't come with a diploma, I also received 15 years of classical music education on the violin and piano, and the simultaneous precision and expressivism that is required of the classical musician continues to shape my creative process today. I am interested in exploring algorithms in Nature and living materials, then combining these with additive manufacturing to design symbiotically. When it comes to design, my philosophy is that:
1) Every product should have a lifespan, a lifecycle, and a continuous path of evolution.
2) We should manufacture additively.
3) We should be symbiotic members of our natural environment; what we consume we must return in some useful form.
