In my free time, I have started teaching myself Houdini. I've found I really enjoy it, and find it to be much more intuitive than Blender! I haven't really tackled the rendering side of things yet, but have been enjoying experimenting with creating geometry.

Modeling Hericium

The way Houdini makes it so easy to visualize recursive grammars via the L-system node makes me wish I'd known about it when I was learning about recursion in school! I thought it would be fun to try and mimic the geometry of the mushrooms in the Hericium family, which includes lion's mane, bear tooth, and a few others. They have a branching structure, and the branches terminate in little tendrils that vary in length and size based on the stage of growth and species, as can be seen in the images below. Below you can see a few examples of Hericium I've found while out foraging (they're edible!) followed by my attempt to mimic their growth pattern in Houdini using an L system node.
